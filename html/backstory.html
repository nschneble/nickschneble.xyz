Who is Nick Schneble?

It all started with Cal Ripken Jr.

My first great love was the bacon cheeseburger from TGI Fridays in the early 2000s. Sum 41's Fat Lip playing on the radio. A pair of American Eagle "distressed" boot cut jeans—bought with my employee discount—torn at the bottoms as they dragged beneath my shoes.

I lived a life in Atlanta. A degree from Georgia Tech. The Philly cheesesteak sandwich from Waffle House after cheap beer at Sidelines. Frosted tips.

I lived another life in LA. Late night stops at In-N-Out. Sunny weekend drives on the PCH. A complete dissociation of the seasons.

In my late twenties and thirties, I went global and spent over a decade traveling as a digital nomad. I co-founded a conference with the goal of visiting all the continents. We made it to five before the pandemic dropped.

Most recently, I've been blessed with late-discovered neurodivergence. Some rather successful antidepressants. A newfound love of mapmaking, pottery, and plant-based cooking. An appreciation of the North Shore of Massachusetts.

I can't wait to see how Dungeon Crawler Carl ends. When Haunted Chocolatier launches and I spend hours in my shop. I might also move to Scotland.
